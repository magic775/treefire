marsa matrouh

Marsa Matrouh, located on Egypt's northwest coast, is considered one of the most beautiful tourist destinations in the country. It is known for its captivating natural beauty, clear turquoise waters, and long stretches of white sandy beaches. The city features unique attractions such as Agiba Beach, with its breathtaking beauty; Al-Ameed Nature Reserve, located nearby; Cleopatra Beach, where it is said that Queen Cleopatra once swam; and the Salt Cave. Marsa Matrouh enjoys a mild climate throughout the year, making it a favored destination for both domestic and international tourism. The city is recognized for its tranquility compared to other tourist cities, offering visitors a chance to escape the hustle and bustle of daily life.
